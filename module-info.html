Module Information and Help

1 Welcome

Welcome to the Transport Data Science module.

Module Leader: Robin Lovelace

Description: This module focuses on applying data science techniques to solve real-world transport problems. It teaches data science and AI-driven code development techniques to solve applied transport problems using a range of transport datasets. This involves origin-destination modelling, routing, mode choice estimation, and geocomputation using open source software. You’ll gain practical skills for sharing code and publishing reproducible results for maximum societal benefit.

Teaching Delivery: Topics will be delivered in a series of weekly sessions in which is a lecture followed by a practical. Two seminars throughout the the module provide an opportunity to apply the methods and techniques to real-world problems, with input from external experts working in the field. Material for lectures will be posted on Minerva and/or this website, usually 48 hours before the lectures.

In the following sections you will find more information about the module.

3 Module Structure

The module consists of 6 lectures and 6 practical sessions, delivered in 3-hour blocks each week. There are also two 3-hour seminars.

See the Schedule for the module timetable, showing the week, class title, tutor, and content of each session.

The assessment structure includes a draft portfolio submission for formative feedback, followed by the final coursework deadline. Key assessments and submission details can be found on the Assessment Overview page.

5 Module Accessibility Statement

  • We have made the contents of the module as accessible as possible long-term, by creating an open-access website that you can navigate to at itsleeds.github.io/tds. The website contains some of the key materials that can be used with assistive technologies, while Minerva contains additional resources, such as datasets and information in additional formats.
  • The assessment on this module involves computer lab work and coursework. Please contact us regarding reasonable adjustments.
  • If you are unable to access any of the content of this module for disability-related reasons, please contact the module leader. You should also ensure that you have registered for support from Disability Services.

6 How to Give Module Feedback

Feedback is important to ensure your module can be improved and developed to provide the best learning experience possible.

  • You can give feedback about your module to your course reps to raise at the Student Staff Partnership Forum. If you are unsure who your course rep is, email academicsupport@luu.leeds.ac.uk.
  • You can speak to your module leader, seminar tutors, or personal tutor.
  • Your module leader might ask for mid-module feedback, and you’ll be asked to complete a module evaluation survey towards the end of the module.

If you have any questions or concerns, please contact the Module Leader. We encourage you to raise issues with the module leader as soon as they arise so that they can be dealt with in a timely manner.

We also welcome your module evaluation at the end of the module. All students at Leeds have the opportunity to offer feedback on their modules. Feedback is important to us and it helps us to make improvements, to understand the student experience, and to see what is already working well.

Your feedback will be used by your module leaders, and sometimes also your seminar tutor or programme leader, to reflect on the delivery of the module and to make improvements.

6.1 Explorance Blue

Explorance Blue is the University of Leeds’ system for processing module evaluations. This section explains how Explorance Blue works in practice.

When your module evaluation goes live, you will receive an email inviting you to log into the system. This link will be unique to you, so please do not share it. 

Module evaluations go live towards the end of a semester. When you have evaluations to complete you will be directed via an individualised link to a page with your open evaluations listed on it.
